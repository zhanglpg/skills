## ⚠️ Source Quality Guidelines

### ✅ High Quality Signals
- Cites primary sources (SEC filings, earnings calls, official data)
- Shows methodology and data sources
- Has track record of accuracy
- Discloses conflicts/positions
- Provides nuanced analysis

### 🚩 Red Flags
- Clickbait headlines ("X Stock Will 10x!")
- No disclosure of positions
- Relies on anonymous sources
- Aggressive paid newsletter promotion
- Consistently wrong without accountability

---

## 🔧 Usage Notes

1. **Cross-reference**: Never rely on a single source
2. **Primary sources first**: Check company filings before secondary analysis
3. **Track record matters**: Follow analysts who admit mistakes
4. **Diversify perspectives**: Mix bullish and bearish viewpoints
5. **Update quarterly**: Review and prune this list
